{{#markdown}} Friends and clients, {{#if version-A}} As a seller, multiple offer situations sound amazing. After all, you can choose which offer you want. Sometimes, the buyers will even drive the price above asking price! However, multiple offer situations can also be overwhelming. If you have five or 10 buyers competing against each other for your property, how do you know which offer is best for you? First of all, keep in mind that the highest offer is not always the best one. Price is certainly an important factor when choosing which offer to accept. However, there are many other factors to consider besides price. For example, do you want to keep your washer, dryer, and refrigerator? It’s also important to know when the buyer wants to take possession of your home, especially if you haven’t found your next property yet. Your timeline needs to line up with the buyer’s. You may need to negotiate a rent-back from the buyer if you are not ready to move yet. On the other hand, the buyer might want to take possession two weeks before you are even scheduled to move. That might not be the best offer to accept. You should also pay attention to the buyer’s financing. Cash is king 99% of the time. When you accept a cash offer, you are not required to do an appraisal, which almost ensures that you will make it to the closing table. Most buyers need a mortgage, though. If you accept a mortgage offer, you need to do an appraisal. If the home doesn’t appraise, the deal could fall through. Finally, pay attention to contingencies. Almost every buyer will request a home inspection, but some add speciality inspections. Others may make an offer that is contingent on the buyer selling their home. Ultimately, the fewer contingencies an offer has, the better off you are.
